If a sample is drawn from a population, a formula must be used to account for margins of error and confidence intervals. When using statistical samples, it is possible to have a lot of knowledge about a population, only have some knowledge, or no knowledge at all. For instance, a population might have a bimodal distribution (as is frequently the case with class grades in mathematics classes), be normally distributed (for heights, weights, or IQs), or have no information about how it will behave (such as polling college students to get their opinions about quality of student life). When nothing is known about a population's behavior, use Slovin's formula.
